When Debt Feels Impossible, You Have Options

Free guide to debt relief, bankruptcy, and when each applies.

If your debt keeps growing faster than you can pay it down, you are not alone — roughly 1 in 7 Americans has been sued by a debt collector, and bankruptcy filings have risen every year since 2022. There are four major relief paths, and the right one depends on your income, assets, and debt type.

Debt management plan (DMP): A nonprofit credit counselor negotiates lower interest rates. You make one monthly payment for 3-5 years. Works for credit card debt when you can afford the principal. Does not touch medical, student loan, or secured debt.

Debt settlement: For-profit companies negotiate lump-sum payoffs at 40-60 cents on the dollar. Tax consequences (forgiven debt = income), and settled accounts hit your credit for 7 years.

Chapter 7 bankruptcy: Most unsecured debts (credit cards, medical, collection accounts) wiped in 3-4 months. 93% success rate. You keep exempt property (home equity, retirement, tools of trade, vehicle up to a limit). Means test required.

Chapter 13 bankruptcy: 3-5 year repayment plan. Keeps your home, saves your car from repossession, strips junior liens off underwater property. Only ~35% complete the plan and get the discharge — choose carefully.

Common Question

How do I know if I should file bankruptcy?

Three tests: (1) is your unsecured debt more than 40% of annual income? (2) would it take more than 5 years to pay off at your current rate? (3) are creditors suing or garnishing? If any two are yes, bankruptcy deserves a serious look.
